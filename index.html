Brand & Social Media Audit

@Cargoboxxpress

Branding & Visual Identity

Develop vertical and icon-only versions of the logo to allow better visual adaptability across different platforms. A full brand guideline with colours, fonts, and logo rules will elevate presentation.

Scenario: When customers see the same visual look on a delivery box, Instagram post, and website, they remember you easier—even after just one order.

Feed Design & Content Layout

Move away from the square-only format and use vertical (4:5) posts to better utilise screen space. Break down complex information into bite-sized carousels. Keep the feed visually calm—less clutter equals more attention.

Tip: Use pinned carousels to act like a storefront window—summarise what you offer and how to order in 3–5 slides.

Engagement & Insights

Low comment activity makes it hard to learn what your audience really wants. Run polls and Q&A in Instagram Stories to directly engage followers. Offer a small incentive (free delivery, branded merch) for completing a 1-minute survey.

Real-world example: One client gave away a keyring and got over 100 survey responses that reshaped their product line.

Competitor Highlights

Each of these competitors has carved a presence in their own way. Not necessarily to copy—but to observe:

  • ASAP Express: Leans heavily into showcasing customer satisfaction through visuals and testimonials.
  • Agape Courier: Uses Reels and explainer content to simplify their service offering for viewers.
  • Reliable Courier JA: Focuses on deal-driven posting and regularity to keep their name top-of-mind.

Suggestions & Insights

Below are a few directional suggestions based on current best practices and competitor activity. These are meant to inspire—not set in stone. The most effective strategy will come once we've had a chance to meet, understand the nuances of your company objectives, and define your unique brand voice together.

Start a "Cargoboxxpress Explained" series—each post answering a different common shipping question visually.
Highlight service areas with geo-tagged photos or graphics to make it clearer where you deliver and collect.
Run a recurring “Delivery of the Week” or “Surprise Shoutout” to boost retention and reward frequent customers.
Where possible, include people—staff, drivers, customers—so your posts feel relatable and warm. Faces make content more human and memorable.
Inject stories. Instead of just saying "we deliver fast," show the journey of a package or a customer's first-time experience. These stories stick with people and get reshared naturally.
Even as a small team, lean into your size. Don’t shy away from being seen. Instead, build an intimate community that roots for you. Feature your founders. Collaborate on posts. Bring followers along your journey of growth—that transparency and authenticity will set you apart in a sea of sameness.
